Books Recommendations

Not sure if anyone else will find these as fascinating as I did, but here are some books I’ve thoroughly enjoyed. To keep things light (and because I get that not everyone loves a dense math book), I’ve left out the super technical reads—but there’s still plenty of fun and insight here.

To me, a book is like an invitation into someone’s mind. Writing is really about how the author feels and how they choose to express those feelings. And that’s the fascinating part—trying to piece together how a person works. You can learn a lot by observing how someone behaves, treats others, or even what they love and hate. But reading their thoughts? That’s where the real mystery unfolds.

You get glimpses of their lessons, regrets, dreams—sometimes it even feels like they’re writing about you. Some books make you nod along in sympathy; others feel like you could’ve written them yourself. And in the process, you end up learning just as much about yourself as you do about the author.

My Stories & Milestones

Okay, full disclosure—this is where things get a little cringey. But hey, we’ve all been there.

Back in 2018, I was wrestling with a lot of emotions, and my genius strategy was to shut them down until they… disappeared? (Spoiler: That didn’t work. Shocking, I know.)

Somehow, in all that chaos, I stumbled onto writing as a way to make sense of things. But I didn’t write about my problems directly—instead, I wrote about the things I loved, the moments that stuck with me, the thoughts that wouldn’t let go. Some of these turned into absurdly long rambles (how did I even have the time?), but looking back, they mattered. They were tiny anchors in the storm.

And then, of course, there was him. Did I write because I’d finally found a way to process things? Or because I’d found a muse worth preserving? I still don’t know. But that’s a question for another poem.
